# Module 6 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 6.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 6 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2 worked examples**.
- Module 6 covers measurement: areas of quadrilaterals and regular polygons, circle areas and sectors, surface area, cross sections, volumes of prisms/pyramids/cylinders/cones/spheres, and density.
- 9 lessons map to 16 instruction days, averaging ~1.75 per lesson.

## Why This Split Works

- `6-1` (area of quadrilaterals) splits naturally: parallelogram/rhombus day then trapezoid/kite day.
- `6-2` (area of regular polygons) splits parts identification and formula application from composite figure day.
- `6-3` (area of circles/sectors) splits circle area day from sector area day; mastery follows both area lessons.
- `6-4` (surface area) has the heaviest load (9 examples across prisms, cylinders, pyramids, cones, spheres, composites) and gets 3 days: prisms/cylinders, pyramids/cones, spheres/composites.
- `6-5` (cross sections) splits symmetry/identification day from solids of revolution/axis symmetry day.
- `6-6` (volume of prisms/pyramids) splits basic volume day from composite volume and frame problems day.
- `6-7` (volume of cylinders/cones/spheres) splits formula computation day from composite/hemisphere day.
- `6-8` (similar solids) splits area ratio day from volume ratio and real-world applications day.
- `6-9` (density) is compact enough to cover in one intensive day with all three example types.
- Four mastery days, one jigsaw, and review/test create sustainable pacing across 9 lessons.

# Unit 2 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 2 (Quadrilaterals).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 2 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 5 textbook lessons and 11 instruction days, lessons 2-1 receives two days; lessons 2-2, 2-3, and 2-4 are each split from their original dense coverage (5-6 worked examples) into two focused days; lesson 2-5 receives three days to separate the parallelogram/rectangle, rhombus, and trapezoid/multi-step coordinate proof clusters.
- A mid-unit review day has been inserted after the parallelogram hierarchy block (lessons 2-1 through 2-3) and before the transition to trapezoids and coordinate proofs.

## Why This Split Works

- `2-1` divides naturally into an interior-angle-sum day and an exterior-angle day with real-world tessellation.
- `2-2` splits into a core properties day (finding missing measures, proving a parallelogram, diagonal bisecting) and an algebraic/coordinate day (algebraic angle equations, distance-formula verification). The original 5-example day is split 3+2: the first day builds the property toolkit, the second day applies it to algebraic and coordinate contexts.
- `2-3` splits into a properties day (distinguishing rectangle/rhombus/square) and a coordinate-proof day.
- `2-4` splits into core trapezoid properties (base angles, midsegment, general angle sums) and deeper applications (isosceles proof using diagonals, real-world multi-step measurements). The original 5-example day is split 3+2: the first day establishes the property vocabulary, the second day demands proof and modeling.
- `2-5` splits into parallelogram/rectangle/rhombus coordinate proofs and trapezoid/multi-step coordinate proofs.
- The mid-unit review (Period 9) sits at the natural boundary between the parallelogram hierarchy block (polygon angles through special quadrilaterals) and the trapezoid/coordinate-proofs block.
- The non-instruction days create room for mastery cycles and mathematical-practices work across the parallelogram hierarchy and trapezoid properties.

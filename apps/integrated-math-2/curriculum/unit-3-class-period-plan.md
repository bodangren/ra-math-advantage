# Unit 3 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 3 (Similarity).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 3 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 5 textbook lessons and 11 instruction days, lesson 3-5 receives two days; lessons 3-1, 3-2, and 3-3 are each split from their original dense coverage (5 worked examples) into two focused days; lesson 3-4 receives three days to separate introductory proofs, proportion-within-proof, and multi-step/overlapping/coordinate proofs.
- A mid-unit review day has been inserted after the similarity criteria block (lessons 3-1 through 3-2) and before the transition to indirect measurement and proofs.

## Why This Split Works

- `3-1` splits into core similar-figures concepts (scale factor, missing sides, similarity determination) and application/dilation (coordinate dilation, map scale). The original 5-example day is split 3+2: the first day establishes the definition and proportional reasoning, the second day extends to transformations and real-world contexts.
- `3-2` splits into an AA/SAS day and an SSS/criterion-selection day, matching the two distinct objective clusters.
- `3-3` splits into core indirect-measurement methods (shadow, river-width, scale drawing) and deeper applications (mirror method, triangle proportionality theorem). The original 5-example day is split 3+2: the first day covers the most common proportional-reasoning methods, the second day introduces the mirror method and formalizes the proportionality theorem.
- `3-4` splits into three days: an introductory proof day (AA proof, proportion-within-proof), a focused parallel-line multi-step proof day, and a multi-step/overlapping/coordinate proof day. This separates the proof-structure building block from the more complex synthesis tasks.
- `3-5` splits into scale-model/blueprint applications and fractal/engineering applications.
- The mid-unit review (Period 6) sits at the natural boundary between the similarity definition/criteria block and the application/proof block.
- The non-instruction days create room for mastery cycles and mathematical-practices work across similarity criteria selection and proof strategy.

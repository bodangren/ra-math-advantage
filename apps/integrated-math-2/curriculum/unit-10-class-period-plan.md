# Unit 10 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 10 (Exponents and Roots).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 10 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 5 textbook lessons and 12 instruction days, lessons 10-1 and 10-2 each receive two days; 10-3 receives two days; 10-4 receives two days; 10-5 receives three days (split from original dense days into basic solving, cube root and multi-radical types, and extraneous solutions with real-world applications).

## Why This Split Works

- `10-1` splits into a product/quotient day (core rules with verification) and a power-rule/combination day that extends to power of a product and scientific notation application.
- `10-2` splits into a negative-exponents day (including rational exponent introduction) and a conversion/simplification day covering radical ↔ exponential form and combined rules.
- `10-3` is split into two days: one for conversion, multiplication, and division (consistent multiply-coefficients/add-exponents pattern) and one focused on addition/subtraction (requires exponent adjustment) plus real-world application.
- `10-5` receives three days across the unit: basic solving with square root and cube root isolation, cube root and both-sides extension, and extraneous solutions with real-world applications (after 10-4).
- `10-4` splits into a simplification day (square roots, cube roots, like radicals, multiplying) and a rationalization day focused on denominators with variables.
- The mid-unit review after the mastery cycle on Day 5 creates a checkpoint on the exponent/rational-exponent foundation before the unit pivots to scientific notation and radicals.
- The non-instruction days create room for one mastery cycle (10a–10d), mathematical-practices work, and a comprehensive review before the assessment.

# Unit 4 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 4 (Right Triangles & Trigonometry).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 4 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 6 textbook lessons and 14 instruction days, lessons receive the following day counts: 4-1 × 2, 4-2 × 2, 4-3 × 2, 4-4 × 2, 4-5 × 3, 4-6 × 3.

## Why This Split Works

- `4-1` splits into core Pythagorean theorem + converse day and a distance-formula / real-world / triples day.
- `4-2` splits into a core special-right-triangle ratios day (30-60-90, 45-45-90, equilateral altitude) and an extensions day (unit circle coordinates, real-world diagonal).
- `4-3` splits into a trig-ratio-definition day (finding sides) and an inverse-trig / real-world / coordinate-point day.
- `4-4` splits into a core solving-right-triangles day (given angle+side, given two sides, depression) and an extensions day (navigation bearings, composite figures).
- `4-5` splits into a Law-of-Sines-introduction day (AAS, area formula), an ambiguous-case / triangulation day, and a complete-oblique-triangle-solution day.
- `4-6` splits into a Law-of-Cosines-foundations day (SAS, SSS), a method-selection / Heron's-formula day, and a real-world-application day.
- The mid-unit review after the jigsaw day gives students a consolidation point before transitioning from right-triangle to oblique-triangle tools.
- The non-instruction days create room for mastery cycles and mathematical-practices work across the right-triangle and oblique-triangle toolkits.

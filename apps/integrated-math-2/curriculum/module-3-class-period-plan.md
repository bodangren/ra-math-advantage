# Module 3 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 3.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 3 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2 worked examples**.
- Module 3 covers similarity: dilations, similar polygons, AA/SSS/SAS similarity, triangle proportionality, and parts of similar triangles.
- 6 lessons map to 12 instruction days, averaging 2 per lesson.

## Why This Split Works

- `3-1` (dilations) is a transformation prerequisite and splits naturally into identification/classification day and graphing/application day.
- `3-2` (similar polygons) is a bridge lesson: identifying parts/proportions day and solving for unknowns/application day.
- `3-3` (AA) is compact enough to pair with `3-4` (SSS/SAS) in the same cluster, both on similarity criteria.
- `3-5` (triangle proportionality) carries a heavier example load (5 examples) and gets a 2-day treatment with midsegment and real-world emphasis on day 2.
- `3-6` (parts of similar triangles) splits into computational day and proof day, with the jigsaw providing mathematical practices work between the two.
- Mastery days land after the similarity criteria cluster and after the proportionality cluster.

# Unit 8 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 8 (Relations and Functions).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 8 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 6 textbook lessons and 13 instruction days, multiple lessons receive two or three days each through splitting. The three additional instruction days come from: 8-2/8-3 separating domain-restriction identification from function-evaluation practice; 8-3 separating composite evaluation from piecewise definitions; and 8-5 separating shifts from reflections/stretches; 8-6 separating table-based comparisons from graph-based and real-world comparisons.

## Why This Split Works

- `8-1` and `8-2` share a natural boundary: domain and range bridge both lessons, so Period 2 combines the tail of 8-1 with the start of 8-2.
- `8-2` splits into an algebraic-restriction day (Ex 3-4: square root and denominator restrictions, mapping diagrams) and a contextual-evaluation day (Ex 5 plus 8-3 Ex 1-2: projectile domain/range, basic function evaluation). The first day isolates the skill of finding restricted domains; the second day applies domain/range in context and introduces function notation.
- `8-3` splits into a core evaluation day (paired with 8-2 on Period 4), a composite evaluation day (Ex 3-4: composite expressions, contextual interpretation), and a piecewise day (Ex 5: piecewise function definitions). The piecewise concept receives focused treatment separate from composite evaluation.
- `8-4` splits into a linearity-identification day (Ex 1-2: constant rate of change test, graph shape) and a rate-of-change-and-modeling day (Ex 3-5: rate calculation, linear vs. exponential, model selection).
- `8-5` splits into a shifts day (Ex 1-2: vertical and horizontal translations) and a reflections/stretches day (Ex 3-4: x-axis reflection, vertical stretch), followed by a description-and-application day (Ex 5-6: combined transformation description, real-world profit function). The split separates foundational shift intuition from the more abstract reflection and stretch concepts.
- `8-6` splits into a table-and-graph comparison day (Ex 1-2: rate of change, y-intercepts) and a deeper comparison day (Ex 3-5: maximum values, representation conversion, phone plan comparison). The first day builds comparison fluency from tabular and graphical data; the second day adds representation conversion and real-world decision-making.
- The mid-unit review and test after Period 16 checkpoint foundational function skills before mastery of transformations and comparison.
- The non-instruction days create room for mastery cycles grouped by objective cluster and mathematical-practices work without forcing a one-to-one match between textbook lessons and class periods.

# Unit 5 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 5 (Circles).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 5 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 5 textbook lessons and 12 instruction days, lessons receive the following day counts: 5-1 × 2, 5-2 × 2, 5-3 × 2, 5-4 × 3, 5-5 × 3.
- The unit includes a mid-unit review after chord properties, providing a consolidation point before tangent/secant and circle equations.

## Why This Split Works

- `5-1` splits into a core circle-vocabulary day (parts, arc length, sector area) and a real-world / radius-diameter day.
- `5-2` splits into a core inscribed-angle-theorem day (inscribed, central, semicircle) and a multi-angle / complex-application day.
- `5-3` splits into a core chord-properties day (chord length, congruent chords, arc-chord relationships) and a perpendicular-bisector / real-world day.
- `5-4` splits into a tangent-perpendicularity / segment-length day, a tangent-tangent-angle / secant-secant day, and a power-of-a-point / real-world day.
- `5-5` splits into a core standard-form day (writing, identifying), a graphing / completing-the-square day, and a three-point / general-form day.
- The mid-unit review after chord properties gives students a consolidation point before transitioning to tangent/secant and circle equations.
- The non-instruction days create room for mastery cycles and mathematical-practices work across the full range of circle theorems and equations.

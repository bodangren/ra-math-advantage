# Unit 7 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 7 (Probability).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 7 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 6 textbook lessons and 14 instruction days, all six lessons receive two days each through splitting (7-1 through 7-6 each split into two days). The three additional days come from splitting three lessons further: 7-2 separates foundational counting principle from advanced tree-diagram and restriction applications; 7-3 separates factorial and nPr basics from repeated-element permutations; 7-4 separates basic nCr calculations from committee-selection applications.

## Why This Split Works

- `7-1` splits into a core probability day (theoretical probability, card probability, complementary events) and a data-interpretation day (frequency tables, weather-forecast multi-step application). The split lets students consolidate ratio-based probability before tackling data-driven contexts.
- `7-2` splits into a foundational counting-principle day (independent events: coin flips, outfits, passwords) and an advanced applications day (tree diagrams for dependent events, counting with restrictions). The first day builds fluency with the multiplication principle; the second day extends to dependent scenarios and constrained counting.
- `7-3` splits into a permutation-foundations day (factorial, nPr introduction), a repeated-elements day (MISSISSIPPI-type problems), and an applications day (race orders, tournament scheduling). The repeated-elements concept receives focused treatment as a distinct skill.
- `7-4` splits into a combinations-introduction day (nCr, permutation-vs-combination), a committee-application day, and a restrictions / Pascal's Triangle day. The committee day gives applied combination problems their own focused treatment.
- `7-5` splits into a P(A and B) day (independent and dependent) and a P(A or B) day (mutually exclusive, inclusion-exclusion, real-world).
- `7-6` splits into a core expected-value day (dice game, fairness) and a real-world-application day (insurance, raffle, decision-making).
- The mid-unit review and test after Period 12 checkpoint counting-methods mastery before students encounter compound probability.
- The non-instruction days create room for mastery cycles, mathematical-practices work, and three review touchpoints (mid-unit, end-of-unit, and final targeted review).

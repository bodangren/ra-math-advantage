This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 13.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 13 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`

- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- Module 13 is the most identity-heavy module in IM2; each identity family is given dedicated instructional time.
- The jigsaw day is placed after Pythagorean identities to deepen the understanding of how identities relate to each other and to the fundamental sine/cosine relationship.

## Why This Split Works

- `13-1` (fundamental identities) and `13-2` (Pythagorean identities) are both identity-family lessons; the mastery day between them consolidates the foundational identity work before the angle-sum identities add complexity.
- `13-3` (sum/difference) and `13-4` (double/half-angle) form a natural progression ‚Äî the double-angle formulas are derived by applying sum formulas with A = B, and half-angle follows from double-angle.
- The jigsaw between `13-2` and `13-3` gives students breathing room to consolidate the Pythagorean identity family before the angle-addition formulas introduce a new structural pattern.
- `13-5` (solving equations) requires fluency with all prior identity types; the three-day treatment allows for restricted domain, unrestricted/general solution, and graphical verification ‚Äî each building on the previous day's skills.

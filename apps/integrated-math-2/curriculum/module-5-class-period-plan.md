# Module 5 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 5.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 5 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2 worked examples**.
- Module 5 covers circles: circumference and arc measure, chords, inscribed angles, tangents, angle measures formed by tangents/secants, equations of circles, and equations of parabolas.
- 8 lessons map to 14 instruction days, averaging 1.75 per lesson.

## Why This Split Works

- `5-1` (circles/circumference) splits into parts identification day and computation/formula day.
- `5-2` (arcs) splits angle/arc classification from radian conversion and arc length.
- `5-3` (arcs and chords) is relatively compact and pairs with `5-4` (inscribed angles) in the same instruction cluster, each getting a day.
- `5-4` (inscribed angles) splits measure-finding day from proof day.
- `5-5` (tangents) splits common tangents/identification from angle measures and circumscribed polygons.
- `5-6` (angle measures with tangents/secants) is a denser lesson and gets 2 days: angle classification/measurement then real-world application and proof.
- `5-7` (equations of circles) splits writing equations from identifying properties and real-world applications.
- `5-8` (parabolas) splits standard form writing from real-world applications, with the jigsaw providing relief between the two circle lessons and two parabola lessons.

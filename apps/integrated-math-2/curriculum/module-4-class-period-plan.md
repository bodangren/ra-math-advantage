# Module 4 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 4.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 4 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2 worked examples**.
- Module 4 covers right triangles and trigonometry: geometric mean, Pythagorean Theorem, 3D coordinates, special right triangles, trigonometry basics, Law of Sines, and Law of Cosines.
- 8 lessons map to 16 instruction days, averaging 2 per lesson.

## Why This Split Works

- `4-1` (geometric mean) is foundational for right triangle similarity and splits into definition/application day and computation/real-world day.
- `4-2` (Pythagorean Theorem) and `4-3` (3D coordinates) each get 2 days: theorem application then classification/extension.
- `4-4` (special right triangles) splits 45-45-90/30-60-90 day from mixed practice and real-world applications.
- `4-5` (trigonometry) splits ratios day from solving/application day, with mastery following.
- `4-6` (applying trigonometry) handles angles of elevation/depression first, then area and two-angle indirect measurement.
- `4-7` (Law of Sines) splits basic application from the critical ambiguous case discussion.
- `4-8` (Law of Cosines) splits formula application from method choice and real-world problems.
- The four mastery days, two jigsaw days, and review/test create a sustainable rhythm across 8 lessons.

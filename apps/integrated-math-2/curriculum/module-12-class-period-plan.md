# Module 12 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 12.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 12 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- This plan was revised against the individual lesson files in `modules/module-12-lesson-*/`.
- Module 12 is the most graph-intensive module in IM2; every lesson connects graphing to algebraic understanding.
- The jigsaw day falls between transformations (which set up vertex form) and the solving methods, which students need vertex form to understand.

## Why This Split Works

- `12-1` (graphing basics) and `12-2` (transformations) are closely related; splitting them allows the key features vocabulary to solidify before transformations are layered on top.
- `12-3` (solving by graphing) introduces the zero/root concept in its most intuitive form before the more formal factoring and completing-the-square methods arrive.
- The jigsaw between `12-3` and `12-4` creates a natural checkpoint: students have seen the graphical interpretation of solutions and are now ready to see why factoring gives the same answers algebraically.
- `12-4` (factoring) and `12-5` (completing the square) together complete the solving-methods arc, with the vertex-form writing as the conceptual payoff of completing the square.

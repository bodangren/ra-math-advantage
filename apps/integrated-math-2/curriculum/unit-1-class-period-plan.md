# Unit 1 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 1 (Relationships in Triangles).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 1 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 6 textbook lessons and 13 instruction days, all six lessons receive two days each, with lessons 1-3, 1-4, and 1-6 split from their original dense single-day coverage (5-6 worked examples) into two focused days. A second round of expansion further split the bridging period (1-1/1-2, formerly 5 examples) and the 1-6 proof day (formerly 4 examples) into separate focused days.
- A mid-unit review day has been inserted after the congruence criteria and jigsaw day, providing a checkpoint before the transition to proofs.

## Why This Split Works

- `1-1` and `1-2` share a natural boundary: the exterior angle theorem bridges both lessons. The former single Period 2 (5 examples) has been split into a 1-1-focused day (exterior angle theorem, real-world application, combining classifications) and a 1-2-focused day (triangle angle sum, exterior angle theorem bridging). This gives students the exterior angle theorem in isolation before layering on angle-sum algebraic problems.
- `1-3` splits into a core triangle inequality day (can/cannot form a triangle, real-world check) and a deeper applications day (third-side range, integer constraints, side-angle ordering). The original single day packed in 6 examples; splitting gives students time to internalize the validity test before tackling range and ordering.
- `1-4` splits into core criteria (SSS, SAS, ASA) and extended criteria (AAS, HL, diagram identification). The original 6-example day is now two focused days: the first establishes the three most common criteria, the second adds AAS and HL plus the synthesis skill of reading diagram markings.
- `1-5` splits into a proofs-introduction day (SAS, ASA, CPCTC) and a multi-step/real-world proof day.
- `1-6` now has three days: Day 1 introduces the base angles theorem, vertex angle relationships, and equilateral properties (Examples 1-3), Day 2 isolates the proof of the base angles theorem (Example 4), and Day 3 extends to multi-step synthesis (inscribed-circle problem, roof truss). The former 4-example day is split into 3 + 1, giving the proof its own focused day.
- The mid-unit review (Period 11) sits at the natural boundary between triangle properties/congruence criteria and the proof block, giving students a checkpoint before the more abstract second half.
- The non-instruction days create room for mastery cycles grouped by objective cluster and mathematical-practices work without forcing a one-to-one match between textbook lessons and class periods.

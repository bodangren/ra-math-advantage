# Module 1 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 1.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 1 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- Module 1 has 7 lessons on triangle relationships: perpendicular bisectors, angle bisectors, medians/altitudes, triangle inequalities, indirect proof, the Triangle Inequality Theorem, and Hinge Theorem.

## Why This Split Works

- `1-1` and `1-2` are natural two-day pairs: properties day followed by application day.
- `1-3` splits cleanly into centroid (median-based) and orthocenter (altitude-based).
- `1-4` divides between ordering/comparison and real-world application.
- `1-5` splits indirect proof into algebraic/geometric day 1 and integer/geometry day 2.
- `1-6` is two days: triangle inequality determination/range, then proof/application.
- `1-7` splits Hinge Theorem introduction from range-finding and proof practice.
- Mastery days land after lessons 1-2, 1-4, and before the final lesson cluster.

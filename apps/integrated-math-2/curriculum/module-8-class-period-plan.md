This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 8.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 8 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- This module benefits from the vertical-line-test and function-notation content being paired for maximum conceptual coherence.
- The jigsaw day is placed after the transformations lesson to deepen understanding of how functions change shape.

## Why This Split Works

- `8-1` and `8-2` share the domain/range theme; splitting them into relations-as-functions and then domain/range deepens the visual and algebraic treatment of each.
- `8-3` (function notation) is a notational bridge that appears throughout the rest of the course; giving it two days ensures students are fluent before moving forward.
- The jigsaw after `8-4` creates deliberate space between the classification of linear vs. nonlinear and the transformations content that follows.
- `8-5` (transformations) is conceptually rich; splitting into shifts and into stretches/reflections lets students build up to combined transformations.
- `8-6` (comparing functions) is the capstone lesson that draws on all prior knowledge; the two-day treatment allows both skill development and contextual synthesis.

# Module 11 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 11.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 11 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- Module 11 is the most procedurally dense module at the Algebra 2 level; factoring in particular benefits from a heavy instructional investment before the solving connection is made.
- The jigsaw day is placed between special products and factoring to deepen conceptual understanding of why factoring patterns work.

## Why This Split Works

- `11-1` (vocabulary) gets two days because the degree/number-of-terms vocabulary is load-bearing for all subsequent communication about polynomials.
- `11-2` through `11-4` build the operations chain (add → subtract → multiply) with the special products as the capstone, creating a coherent arithmetic arc before the factoring methods are introduced.
- The jigsaw between `11-5` (factoring techniques) and the solving day (`11-6`) creates cognitive space to reflect on why factoring patterns exist and how they relate to the distributive property — essential for the conceptual leap to solving.
- `11-6` spans three instruction days because solving polynomial equations is the key application that connects factoring to the rest of Algebra 2; the writing-from-zeros skill is an important extension.

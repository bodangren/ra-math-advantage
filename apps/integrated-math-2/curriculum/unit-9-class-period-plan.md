# Unit 9 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 9 (Linear Equations, Inequalities & Systems).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 9 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 5 textbook lessons and 13 instruction days, three lessons receive two days each through splitting (9-1, 9-4, and 9-5) and three additional splits expand coverage: 9-2 separates core graphing forms from point-slope and line relationships; 9-3 separates compound-inequality foundations from two-variable inequality graphing; 9-4/9-5 separates system classification from inequality-system introduction.

## Why This Split Works

- `9-1` splits into a core solving day (Ex 1-3: distributive property, variables on both sides, fractions) and a special-cases day (Ex 4-5: no solution/infinite solutions, break-even). The first day builds procedural fluency; the second day focuses on classification and application.
- `9-2` splits into a core graphing day (Ex 1-3: slope-intercept, standard form, two points), a forms-and-relationships day (Ex 4-5: point-slope, parallel/perpendicular), and a real-world application day (Ex 6: distance-time model). The third day gives the contextual modeling example focused treatment.
- `9-3` splits into an inequality-foundations day (Ex 1-2: one-variable solving, sign-flip rule), a compound-inequality day (Ex 3-4: AND, OR), and a two-variable day (Ex 5-6: two-variable graphing, budget constraint). The split separates one-variable compound inequalities from the distinct skill of two-variable inequality graphing.
- `9-4` splits into a methods day (Ex 1-2: graphing and substitution), an elimination day (Ex 3-4: add and multiply-first elimination), and a classification/mixture day (Ex 5-6: no solution/infinite solutions, mixture problem).
- `9-5` splits into an introduction day (Ex 1-2: graph inequality systems, identify solution region) and a deeper-application day (Ex 3-5: no-solution systems, point testing, linear programming). The split gives students an entry point to inequality systems before tackling linear programming.
- The mid-unit review after Period 10 checkpoint ensures students have mastered equations, graphing, and inequalities before moving to systems.
- The non-instruction days create room for mastery cycles grouped by objective cluster and mathematical-practices work without forcing a one-to-one match between textbook lessons and class periods.

# Unit 11 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 11 (Polynomials).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 11 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 6 textbook lessons and 14 instruction days, lessons 11-1, 11-2, and 11-3 each receive two days (split from original dense days); 11-4 receives three days (split from original dense day into PST patterns, difference of squares with pattern recognition, and composite PST with GCF); 11-5 receives two days; 11-6 receives two days.

## Why This Split Works

- `11-1` splits into a core-classification day (degree, leading coefficient, standard form) and an extension day (polynomial test and volume modeling).
- `11-2` splits into a core-arithmetic day (add two, subtract, add three) and an extension day (nested parentheses and area applications).
- `11-3` splits into a core-multiplication day (monomial distribution, FOIL, binomial × trinomial) and an extended day (two trinomials and area applications).
- `11-4` splits into three days: a foundational patterns day (PST and difference of squares), an extension day (pattern recognition, mental math, and composite PST), and a GCF/grouping day paired with the start of 11-5. This three-way split gives focused treatment to each special product skill.
- `11-5` splits across three days: GCF and grouping (Day 11), trinomial factoring (Day 12), and difference of squares with complete factoring (Day 13).
- `11-6` receives two days: one for zero product property solving and one dedicated to cubic equations and real-world zero-finding.
- The mid-unit review after Day 6 creates a checkpoint on polynomial operations (11a–11b) before the unit pivots to special products and factoring.
- The non-instruction days create room for two mastery cycles (11a–11b and 11c–11h), mathematical-practices work, a comprehensive review, and two assessment opportunities.

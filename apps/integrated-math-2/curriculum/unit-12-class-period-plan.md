This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 12 (Quadratic Functions).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 12 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 5 textbook lessons and 13 instruction days, 12-1 receives two days; 12-2 receives three days (split from original dense day into leading coefficient 1, leading coefficient ‚âÝ 1, and difference of squares/factorability); 12-3 receives two days; 12-4 receives three days (split from original dense day); 12-5 receives three days (split from original dense day into projectile/revenue, area optimization, and structure modeling).

## Why This Split Works

- `12-1` splits into a core graphing day (vertex identification and equation writing) and a transformations day (describing shifts/reflections, finding max/min, and projectile modeling).
- `12-2` splits into three days: a basic-factoring day (leading coefficient 1), an extended-factoring day (leading coefficient ‚âÝ 1), and a difference-of-squares/factorability day (pattern recognition, discriminant check, and geometric application). This three-way split ensures students master the simpler case before tackling the AC method.
- `12-3` splits into a basic completing-the-square day (simple expressions, solving, and a=1 conversion) and an advanced day (a‚âÝ1 cases and vertex finding).
- `12-4` splits into three days: a formula introduction day (quadratic formula and complex solutions), a discriminant/method day (discriminant analysis and method selection), and a derivation day (formula derivation from completing the square). This three-way split gives focused treatment to each synthesis topic.
- `12-5` splits into three days: a projectile/revenue day, an area optimization day, and a structure modeling day. This three-way split separates motion, geometric, and structural contexts.
- The mid-unit review after Day 7 creates a checkpoint on vertex form, transformations, and factoring (12a‚Äì12c) before the unit pivots to completing the square and the quadratic formula.
- The non-instruction days create room for one mastery cycle (12a‚Äì12c), mathematical-practices work, a comprehensive review, and two assessment opportunities.

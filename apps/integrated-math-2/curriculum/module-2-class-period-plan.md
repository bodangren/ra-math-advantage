# Module 2 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 2.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 2 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2 worked examples**.
- Module 2 covers quadrilaterals: polygon angles, parallelogram properties and tests, rectangle/rhombus/square properties, and trapezoid/kite properties.
- 6 lessons map to 12 instruction days, averaging 2 per lesson.

## Why This Split Works

- `2-1` splits naturally: interior angles day (sum, individual angle, n given angle) then exterior angles day.
- `2-2` and `2-3` are paired: properties/tests of parallelograms split into finding measures day and coordinate proof day.
- `2-4` (rectangles) and `2-5` (rhombi/squares) each split into properties day and coordinate/classification day.
- `2-6` (trapezoids/kites) carries a heavier load with 6 examples spread across 2.5 days, with the jigsaw day providing relief before the final trapezoid/kite mastery and classification day.
- Mastery days land after parallelogram cluster and after rectangle/rhombus/square cluster.

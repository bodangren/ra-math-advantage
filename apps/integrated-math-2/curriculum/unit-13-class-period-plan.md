# Unit 13 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Unit 13 (Trigonometric Identities & Equations).

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Unit 13 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- A few objective-isolating days use **1 anchor example** plus guided practice, discussion, or error analysis.
- Some course objectives are treated as **embedded objectives** rather than the single primary objective for a day.
- With 5 textbook lessons and 12 instruction days, 13-1 receives two days (split from original dense day into reciprocal identities and quotient/verification); 13-2 receives two days; 13-3 receives three days (split from original dense day into sine sum, cosine difference with algebraic simplification, and tangent sum with verification); 13-4 receives two days; 13-5 receives three days (split).

## Why This Split Works

- `13-1` splits into a reciprocal-identities day (core simplification of sin · csc and tan / sec) and a verification/evaluation day (identity verification, sin/cos rewriting, and partial-information evaluation). This two-way split separates procedural simplification from reasoning-intensive verification.
- `13-2` splits into a fundamentals day (finding cos from sin, simplifying, verifying the Pythagorean identity) and an applications day (complex fraction simplification and quadrant-aware trig value finding with tan given).
- `13-3` splits into three days: a sine-sum day (exact value computation with sin(45° + 30°)), a cosine-difference day (cos 15° and algebraic simplification), and a tangent/verification day (tangent sum identity and numerical verification). This three-way split gives focused treatment to each compound-angle identity.
- `13-4` splits into a double-angle day (sin and cos double-angle formulas with all three forms) and a half-angle day (half-angle evaluation, power-reducing, and derivation from sum formulas).
- `13-5` splits into three days: a basic-solving day (reference angles and quadratic-type), a tangent/double-angle day (tangent equations and double-angle equations), and an advanced-solving day (general solutions and graphical methods). This three-way split gives focused treatment to each solving strategy.
- The mid-unit review after Day 15 creates a checkpoint on identity foundations (13a–13e) before the unit pivots to equation solving.
- The non-instruction days create room for one mastery cycle (13a–13g), mathematical-practices work, two reviews, and two assessment opportunities.

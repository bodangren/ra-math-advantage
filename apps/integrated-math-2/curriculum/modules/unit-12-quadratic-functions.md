# Unit 12: Quadratic Functions

## Overview

Students explore quadratic functions in depth — graphing in vertex form, factoring and solving quadratic equations, completing the square, using the quadratic formula, and applying quadratic models to real-world problems involving projectile motion, optimization, and area.

## Lessons

### 12-1 Graphing Quadratics in Vertex Form

Graph $y = a(x-h)^2 + k$. Identify vertex $(h, k)$ and axis of symmetry $x = h$. Determine direction of opening from sign of $a$. Describe transformations from the parent function $y = x^2$. Find maximum or minimum values.

### 12-2 Factoring Quadratics

Review and extend factoring techniques for quadratic expressions. Factor and solve quadratic equations. Determine whether a quadratic can be factored over the integers. Apply factoring to find dimensions and solve geometric problems.

### 12-3 Completing the Square

Complete the square for expressions of the form $x^2 + bx$. Convert quadratic functions from standard form to vertex form. Solve quadratic equations by completing the square. Handle cases where the leading coefficient $a \neq 1$.

### 12-4 Quadratic Formula and the Discriminant

Derive and apply the quadratic formula $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$. Use the discriminant $b^2 - 4ac$ to determine the number and type of solutions. Choose the most efficient method for solving a given quadratic.

### 12-5 Applications of Quadratic Models

Model projectile motion with quadratic functions. Solve optimization problems (maximum area, maximum revenue). Find when a projectile reaches a given height. Analyze parabolic shapes in architecture and engineering.

## Skills Developed

- Graphing quadratic functions and identifying key features
- Factoring and solving quadratic equations
- Completing the square to convert between forms
- Applying the quadratic formula and interpreting the discriminant
- Modeling and solving real-world problems with quadratics

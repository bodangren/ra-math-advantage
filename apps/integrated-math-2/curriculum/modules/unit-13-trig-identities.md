# Unit 13: Trigonometric Identities & Equations

## Overview

Students develop fluency with trigonometric identities — fundamental, Pythagorean, sum/difference, and double/half-angle — and use them to simplify expressions, verify identities, and solve trigonometric equations algebraically and graphically.

## Lessons

### 13-1 Fundamental Trig Identities

Apply reciprocal identities (csc, sec, cot) and quotient identities (tan = sin/cos, cot = cos/sin). Simplify trigonometric expressions. Verify identities using fundamental relationships. Rewrite expressions in terms of sin and cos only.

### 13-2 Pythagorean Identities

Apply $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$ and its variants $\tan^2\theta + 1 = \sec^2\theta$ and $1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta$. Simplify expressions. Find missing trig values. Solve equations using Pythagorean identities.

### 13-3 Sum and Difference Identities

Apply $\sin(A \pm B)$, $\cos(A \pm B)$, and $\tan(A \pm B)$ formulas. Find exact values of trig functions at non-standard angles. Verify sum/difference identities with known angles. Simplify compound angle expressions.

### 13-4 Double-Angle and Half-Angle Identities

Apply $\sin(2\theta)$, $\cos(2\theta)$ (all three forms), and half-angle identities. Simplify expressions. Find exact values using double and half angles. Verify identities.

### 13-5 Solving Trigonometric Equations

Solve trig equations on restricted and unrestricted domains. Use algebraic manipulation and identities. Find all solutions (general form). Use graphical methods to verify solutions.

## Skills Developed

- Simplifying trigonometric expressions using identities
- Verifying trigonometric identities
- Finding exact values using sum, difference, double, and half-angle formulas
- Solving trigonometric equations algebraically and graphically
- Applying identities to real-world periodic phenomena

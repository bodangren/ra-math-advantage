# Unit 11: Polynomials

## Overview

Students work with polynomial expressions — classifying, adding, subtracting, multiplying, and factoring them. They learn special product patterns, develop fluency with factoring techniques, and solve polynomial equations using the zero product property.

## Lessons

### 11-1 Polynomial Vocabulary

Identify degree, leading coefficient, and number of terms. Classify polynomials as monomial, binomial, trinomial, or polynomial. Write polynomials in standard form. Determine whether an expression is a polynomial.

### 11-2 Adding and Subtracting Polynomials

Combine like terms to add and subtract polynomials. Distribute negatives through parentheses. Add three or more polynomials. Apply addition of polynomials to find total area.

### 11-3 Multiplying Polynomials

Multiply monomials by polynomials using the distributive property. Use FOIL for binomial × binomial. Multiply binomials by trinomials and any two polynomials. Apply multiplication to area problems.

### 11-4 Special Products

Recognize and apply perfect square trinomial patterns: $(a+b)^2$ and $(a-b)^2$. Use the difference of squares pattern: $(a+b)(a-b)$. Apply special products to mental math and algebraic simplification.

### 11-5 Factoring Techniques

Factor out the greatest common factor (GCF). Factor by grouping. Factor trinomials with leading coefficient 1 and with leading coefficient ≠ 1. Factor difference of squares. Factor completely using multiple techniques.

### 11-6 Solving Polynomial Equations by Factoring

Apply the zero product property to solve factored equations. Factor first, then solve. Handle equations requiring GCF extraction. Solve higher-degree polynomial equations. Apply to real-world zero-finding problems.

## Skills Developed

- Classifying polynomials by degree and number of terms
- Performing arithmetic operations on polynomials
- Multiplying polynomials using distributive property and FOIL
- Recognizing and applying special product patterns
- Factoring polynomials using multiple techniques
- Solving polynomial equations via the zero product property

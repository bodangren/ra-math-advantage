# Unit 3: Similarity

## Overview

Students develop understanding of similar figures, triangle similarity criteria, and proportional reasoning used in indirect measurement and real-world applications.

## Lessons

### 3-1 Similar Figures and Scale Factors

Defining similarity, identifying corresponding parts, computing and applying scale factors.

### 3-2 Triangle Similarity (AA, SAS, SSS)

Triangle similarity postulates and theorems: Angle-Angle, Side-Angle-Side, and Side-Side-Side.

### 3-3 Proportions and Indirect Measurement

Using similar triangles and proportions to measure heights, distances, and dimensions indirectly (shadow method, mirror method, scale drawings).

### 3-4 Similarity Proofs

Writing two-column and paragraph proofs that establish triangle similarity and use proportional relationships.

### 3-5 Applications of Similarity

Scale models, photography enlargements, blueprint reading, fractals, and engineering applications.

## Skills Developed

- Identifying and proving similarity in geometric figures
- Setting up and solving proportions from similar figures
- Indirect measurement techniques
- Translating real-world problems into similarity relationships

# Unit 6: Measurement

## Overview

Students extend their understanding of area, surface area, and volume to two-dimensional polygons and three-dimensional solids including prisms, pyramids, cylinders, cones, and spheres.

## Lessons

### 6-1 Area of Polygons

Area formulas for triangles, parallelograms, trapezoids, and regular polygons using apothems.

### 6-2 Surface Area of Solids

Surface area of prisms, pyramids, cylinders, cones, and spheres using nets and formulas.

### 6-3 Volume of Prisms and Cylinders

Volume formula $V = Bh$ applied to rectangular prisms, triangular prisms, and cylinders.

### 6-4 Volume of Cones and Spheres

Volume of cones ($V = \frac{1}{3}Bh$), spheres ($V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$), and composite solids.

### 6-5 Cross-Sections and Composite Solids

Identifying cross-sections of 3D figures, Cavalieri's principle, and computing composite volumes.

## Skills Developed

- Computing area of polygons using standard formulas
- Calculating surface area of 3D solids
- Applying volume formulas to prisms, cylinders, cones, and spheres
- Analyzing cross-sections of three-dimensional figures
- Solving real-world measurement problems

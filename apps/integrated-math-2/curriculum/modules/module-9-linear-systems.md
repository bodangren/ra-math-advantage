# Unit 9: Linear Equations, Inequalities & Systems

## Overview

Students solve multi-step linear equations, graph linear equations in multiple forms, solve and graph linear inequalities, solve systems of equations using three methods, and analyze systems of inequalities to find feasible regions.

## Lessons

### 9-1 Solving Multi-Step Linear Equations

Apply distributive property, combine like terms, and move variables to one side. Identify equations with no solution or infinitely many solutions.

### 9-2 Graphing Linear Equations

Graph using slope-intercept, standard, and point-slope form. Convert between forms. Write equations from two points. Identify parallel and perpendicular lines.

### 9-3 Solving and Graphing Linear Inequalities

Solve one-variable and two-variable linear inequalities. Flip the inequality when multiplying or dividing by a negative. Graph compound inequalities (AND/OR) and shaded regions.

### 9-4 Systems of Equations

Solve systems by graphing, substitution, and elimination. Classify systems as having one solution, no solution, or infinitely many solutions.

### 9-5 Systems of Inequalities

Graph systems of linear inequalities. Identify and shade solution regions. Determine if a point lies in the feasible region. Apply to linear programming.

## Skills Developed

- Solving multi-step linear equations
- Graphing linear equations in multiple forms
- Solving and graphing linear inequalities
- Solving systems of equations (graphing, substitution, elimination)
- Graphing and interpreting systems of inequalities
- Applying linear models to real-world problems

# Unit 2: Quadrilaterals

## Overview

Students develop understanding of quadrilateral properties, classifications, and coordinate geometry proofs involving parallelograms, trapezoids, and special quadrilaterals.

## Lessons

### 2-1 Polygon Basics & Angle Sums

Polygon angle sum theorem ((n-2)×180°), interior and exterior angles, regular polygons.

### 2-2 Parallelogram Properties

Opposite sides and angles congruent, diagonals bisect each other, conditions for proving a quadrilateral is a parallelogram.

### 2-3 Special Parallelograms (Rectangle, Rhombus, Square)

Properties of rectangles (all 90°), rhombuses (all sides congruent, perpendicular diagonals), and squares (both properties combined).

### 2-4 Trapezoids and Isosceles Trapezoids

Trapezoid properties, isosceles trapezoid base angles and diagonals, midsegment theorem.

### 2-5 Coordinate Proofs with Quadrilaterals

Using slope, distance, and midpoint formulas to prove quadrilateral types on the coordinate plane.

## Skills Developed

- Classification and property analysis of quadrilaterals
- Algebraic reasoning with polygon angle relationships
- Coordinate geometry proof techniques
- Application of quadrilateral properties to real-world design problems

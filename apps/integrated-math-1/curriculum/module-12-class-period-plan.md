## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 12 (Logical Arguments and Line Relationships) is the longest IM1 module with 10 lessons, covering conjectures/counterexamples, conditional logic, deductive reasoning, proofs, parallel lines, slope, and perpendicular distance.
- Lessons 12-1 through 12-6 form a logical reasoning and proof progression; Lessons 12-7 through 12-10 focus on parallel and perpendicular line relationships.

## Why This Split Works

- `12-1` and `12-2` (conjectures and conditionals) form the foundations of mathematical reasoning ‚Äî pattern recognition and logical connectives.
- `12-3` and `12-4` (deductive reasoning and proofs) extend logical reasoning into formal proof writing.
- `12-5` and `12-6` (segment and angle proof properties) provide the specific properties needed for proof writing.
- `12-7` through `12-9` (parallel lines, transversals, slope) form a cohesive unit on line relationships ‚Äî angle pairs, parallel line theorems, slope equations.
- `12-10` (perpendicular distance) concludes the module with an application of all previous coordinate geometry skills.
- The module has more instruction days to accommodate its 10 lessons and proof-heavy content.
- Frequent mastery days support the sequential reasoning development.

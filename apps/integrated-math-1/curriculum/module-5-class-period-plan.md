## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 5 is the shortest instructional module with 6 lessons; it focuses heavily on writing equations from various given forms.
- Lessons 5-3 (scatter plots) and 5-5 (linear regression) both involve data modeling; Lesson 5-4 (correlation and causation) serves as a conceptual bridge between them.
- The inverse functions lesson (5-6) revisits the switch-and-solve process first introduced in solving literal equations.

## Why This Split Works

- `5-1` splits naturally into writing equations (5a) and modeling/checking (5b), with a mastery day between them.
- `5-2` separates form conversion skills (5c) from parallel/perpendicular reasoning (5d), with the jigsaw day reinforcing both.
- `5-3` dedicates one day to correlation identification (5e) and one to line-of-fit writing and prediction (5f), a natural division.
- `5-4` stands alone as a conceptual lesson (5g) on correlation vs. causation, which benefits from the nearby jigsaw day in period 6.
- `5-5` separates regression technology use (5h) from residual analysis (5i), keeping these distinct statistical skills on separate days.
- `5-6` splits finding inverses algebraically (5j) from graphing and contextual interpretation (5k), reinforcing both the procedural and conceptual aspects of inverses.
- The module's shorter length is reflected in its budget; non-instruction days are concentrated to maximize instructional time.

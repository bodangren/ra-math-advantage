# Module 12: Logical Arguments and Line Relationships

## Overview
This module develops formal geometric reasoning from conjecture-making through proof writing. Students learn to distinguish inductive from deductive reasoning, apply the Law of Detachment and Law of Syllogism, write conditional statements and their converses, and construct two-column, flow, and paragraph proofs. The module applies these skills to parallel and perpendicular lines, including slope relationships, angle theorems for parallel lines cut by a transversal, and finding distances between lines.

## Skills Developed
- Recognize patterns and write conjectures; find counterexamples to disprove false conjectures
- Determine truth values of compound statements (conjunction, disjunction, negation)
- Identify hypothesis and conclusion in conditional statements; write converses, inverses, and contrapositives
- Apply the Law of Detachment and Law of Syllogism to draw valid conclusions
- Write two-column proofs, flow proofs, and paragraph proofs using postulates and properties
- Use reflexive, symmetric, and transitive properties of segment and angle congruence
- Apply the Angle Addition Postulate and properties of linear pairs, supplementary, and complementary angles
- Classify angle pairs (corresponding, alternate interior, alternate exterior, consecutive interior)
- Use parallel line angle theorems to find unknown angle measures
- Calculate slope and write equations in slope-intercept and point-slope forms
- Determine whether lines are parallel or perpendicular by comparing slopes
- Prove lines parallel using converse theorems
- Find the distance from a point to a line and between parallel lines

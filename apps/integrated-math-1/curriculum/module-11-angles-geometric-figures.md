# Module 11: Angles and Geometric Figures

## Overview
This module connects angle relationships with rigid motions and three-dimensional geometry. Students investigate angle notation, bisectors, vertical angles, and linear pairs; explore perimeter, circumference, and area of two-dimensional figures; perform reflections, translations, and rotations on the coordinate plane; classify and measure three-dimensional solids; and use orthographic drawings and nets to represent 3D shapes. The module concludes with precision, accuracy, and significant figures as they apply to real measurement situations.

## Skills Developed
- Name and identify parts of angles, including vertex, sides, interior, and exterior
- Apply the Angle Addition Postulate and the definition of angle bisector
- Identify adjacent angles, vertical angles, linear pairs, complementary, and supplementary angles
- Solve equations using angle relationships
- Calculate perimeter, circumference, and area of polygons and circles
- Identify and perform reflections, translations, and rotations on the coordinate plane
- Classify three-dimensional figures as polyhedra or non-polyhedra
- Calculate surface area and volume of prisms, cylinders, pyramids, and cones
- Construct and interpret orthographic drawings and nets
- Distinguish between accuracy and precision; calculate approximate error and possible ranges
- Apply rules for significant figures in measurements and calculations

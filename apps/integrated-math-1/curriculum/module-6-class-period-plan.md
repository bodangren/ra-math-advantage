## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 6 extends equation-solving skills to inequality contexts, building from one-step to two-variable graphing.
- The critical rule—reversing the inequality symbol when multiplying or dividing by a negative—is emphasized throughout.
- Lesson 6-4 (absolute value inequalities) requires the compound inequality conversion as a prerequisite skill.

## Why This Split Works

- `6-1` splits graphing/writing inequalities (6a) from solving techniques (6b), with a mastery day between them.
- `6-2` gives two days to multi-step inequalities: computational fluency (6c) and special solution reasoning (6d).
- The jigsaw at period 6 breaks up the inequality units and allows consolidation before compound inequalities.
- `6-3` splits solving compound inequalities (6e) from graphing and writing from graphs (6f), keeping these as distinct skills.
- `6-4` splits the less-than and greater-than cases (6g) from special cases and fractional coefficients (6h), which require careful sign analysis.
- `6-5` splits into graphing and writing inequalities (6i) and special cases/test point reasoning (6j), with an additional period (6k) for systems of inequalities from graphs — this extends the lesson's natural content given its connection to Module 7.
- Two review days are allocated because of the volume of solving technique vocabulary (inequality vs. compound vs. absolute value) that students must distinguish.

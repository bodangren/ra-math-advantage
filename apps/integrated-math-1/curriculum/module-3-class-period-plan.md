## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 3 introduces function analysis vocabulary that persists throughout IM1-IM3.

## Why This Split Works

- `3-1` splits naturally into representation methods (3a) and variable/axis interpretation (3b), with a nearby mastery day.
- `3-2` separates function identification (3c) from function evaluation and interpretation (3d), using a jigsaw to reinforce representation flexibility.
- `3-3` divides into discrete/continuous classification (3e) and linearity determination (3f) with a mastery day between them.
- `3-4` splits intercept work (3g) from equation-solving by graphing and zero interpretation (3h), a natural division that keeps graphical analysis fresh.
- `3-5` splits symmetry/increasing-decreasing (3i) from extrema and end behavior (3j), keeping these related but distinct skills on separate days.
- `3-6` uses two days to build from linear sketching (3k) to function comparison and business modeling (3l), allowing depth in applied work.
- The non-instruction days maintain a regular mastery cadence throughout the module.

# Module 1 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 1.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 1 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 1 introduces foundational vocabulary and skills that other modules build upon.
- Some lessons are dense and span 2 instruction days; lighter lessons combine into a single day.
- Real-world application examples are emphasized throughout to reinforce modeling skills.

## Why This Split Works

- `1-1` and `1-2` are both content-rich lessons with multiple example types; they each get two instruction days with a mastery day between them.
- `1-3` divides naturally into properties of equality (day 1) and properties of real numbers (day 2) with a nearby mastery day.
- `1-4` is dense enough to warrant two instruction days, with the jigsaw following to consolidate Distributive Property work.
- `1-5` splits into writing absolute value expressions (modeling) and evaluating them (computation), sharing a mastery day.
- `1-6` covers modeling with ratios/metrics and reasonable accuracy; the two-day split isolates conceptual modeling from computational accuracy.
- The non-instruction days create space for long-term mastery development and mathematical practices work without a one-to-one match between textbook lessons and class periods.

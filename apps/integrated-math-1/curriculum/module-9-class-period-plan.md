## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 9 (Statistics) builds from descriptive statistics (center, spread, display) to statistical reasoning (bias, sampling, comparing data sets) to categorical data analysis (two-way tables).
- Lessons 9-1 through 9-5 form the core descriptive statistics sequence; Lessons 9-6 and 9-7 extend into comparison and categorical analysis.

## Why This Split Works

- `9-1` and `9-2` form the descriptive statistics foundation with measures of center and data displays.
- `9-3` introduces critical statistical reasoning about bias ‚Äî essential before students can properly analyze data.
- `9-4` extends to measures of spread (range, IQR, standard deviation) and visual displays (box plots).
- `9-5` brings shape analysis (symmetric vs. skewed) together with the choice of appropriate statistics.
- `9-6` addresses transformations of data and comparison of two data sets ‚Äî practical applications.
- `9-7` on two-way tables is its own topic and ends naturally before a test.
- The jigsaw day provides practice with real-world statistical analysis and detecting misleading data.

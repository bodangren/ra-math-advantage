## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 14 (Triangles and Congruence) covers triangle angle sum, congruent triangles, SSS/SAS/ASA/AAS/HL congruence criteria, isosceles/equilateral triangles, and coordinate proofs.
- Lessons 14-1 through 14-4 focus on triangle properties and congruence postulates/theorems; Lessons 14-5 through 14-7 extend to special triangle types and coordinate proofs.

## Why This Split Works

- `14-1` (triangle angle sum) is foundational vocabulary ‚Äî interior angles and exterior angle theorem are new for IM1.
- `14-2` and `14-3` (congruent triangles) build toward the five congruence criteria ‚Äî SSS and SAS come first as they require fewer angle references.
- `14-4` (ASA and AAS) extends the congruence criteria ‚Äî these are natural successors to SSS/SAS and use similar proof structure.
- `14-5` (right triangle congruence) is its own special case ‚Äî the four right triangle theorems (LL, HA, LA, HL) deserve focused attention.
- `14-6` (isosceles and equilateral) connects angle and side relationships within triangles ‚Äî the theorem/converse structure parallels the conditional logic from Module 12.
- `14-7` (coordinate proofs) applies all previous coordinate geometry skills (Distance Formula, midpoint, slope) to triangle proofs.
- The non-instruction days provide spaced practice for the proof-writing skills that develop across the module.

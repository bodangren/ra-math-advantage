## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 7 is the final IM1 module, bringing together equation-solving, inequality reasoning, and function knowledge into systems contexts.
- Lesson 7-5 (systems of inequalities) extends Module 6's two-variable inequality graphing into multi-constraint systems.
- The three elimination methods (addition, subtraction, multiplication) build sequentially and are best taught with time between them.

## Why This Split Works

- `7-1` splits graphing and classification (7a) from real-world modeling and decimal systems (7b), with a mastery day between them.
- `7-2` uses two days for substitution: computational fluency (7c) and contextual/fraction/decimals applications (7d), keeping the jigsaw day nearby for method comparison.
- `7-3` splits elimination addition/subtraction (7e) from subtraction and simplification (7f), reflecting the two distinct scenarios for this method.
- `7-4` uses two days for elimination with multiplication: the core multiplication strategy (7g) and the strategic choice of which variable/equations to multiply (7h).
- `7-5` splits into graphing systems of inequalities (7i) and writing/verifying systems (7j), with a third day (7k) for optimization problems that require the full arc of skills.
- Two review days and two test days are allocated because Module 7 consolidates the entire IM1 course, and students benefit from additional synthesis time at the end.
- The jigsaw day at period 6 is positioned after both substitution (7-2) and before elimination (7-3), allowing students to compare all three major methods.

# Module 8 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 8.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Lesson 8-4 (Transforming Exponential Expressions) is lighter and combines with Lesson 8-5 in one instruction day.
- Lesson 8-6 (Recursive Formulas) spans two instruction days due to the variety of example types.

## Why This Split Works

- `8-1` naturally splits into an identification day and a features/application day.
- `8-2` divides between describing transformations and writing equations from graphs.
- `8-3` carries the two-point method and compound interest ‚Äî both are dense enough to justify two days.
- `8-4` is light and pairs efficiently with the beginning of `8-5`.
- `8-5` and `8-6` share the geometric-sequence thread; `8-6` extends into recursive thinking.
- The non-instruction days create space for mastery consolidation and mathematical practices work.

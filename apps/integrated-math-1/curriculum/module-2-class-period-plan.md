# Module 2 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 2.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 2 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `measure/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 2 builds from writing equations (Lesson 2-1) through increasingly complex solving techniques.
- Literal equations and formulas (Lesson 2-7) are an important bridge to geometric and scientific applications.
- Dimensional analysis in Lesson 2-7 introduces a skill that repeats throughout the course.

## Why This Split Works

- `2-1` splits into writing equations (2a) and interpreting/verbalizing equations (2b) with a mastery day following.
- `2-2` dedicates day 1 to solving techniques and day 2 to real-world modeling applications.
- `2-3` and `2-4` are both multi-concept lessons; each gets two days to separate computational fluency from classification and geometric application.
- `2-5` splits into solving (2i) and graphing/writing from graphs (2j) with a shared jigsaw day nearby.
- `2-6` separates proportion solving (2k) from real-world percent and mixture applications (2l).
- `2-7` splits formula rearrangement (2m) from dimensional analysis (2n), acknowledging that unit conversion is a distinct skill set.
- The non-instruction days maintain a regular mastery cycle without forcing a one-to-one match between lessons and periods.

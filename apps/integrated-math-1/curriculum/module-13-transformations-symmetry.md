# Module 13: Transformations and Symmetry

## Overview
This module explores rigid motions and symmetry as fundamental geometric concepts. Students perform reflections, translations, and rotations on the coordinate plane using coordinate rules, investigate compositions of transformations including glide reflections, determine whether figures tessellate the plane, and analyze line and rotational symmetry. The module connects transformations to congruence and to the creation of repeating patterns.

## Skills Developed
- Graph reflections in the x-axis, y-axis, and lines y=x and y=-x
- Reflect figures in horizontal and vertical lines not on the axes
- Determine translation vectors and apply translations to find image coordinates
- Verify that a transformation is a translation by checking consistent vector shifts
- Rotate figures 90, 180, and 270 degrees about the origin and about arbitrary points
- Graph and identify glide reflections and other compositions of transformations
- Determine the single transformation equivalent to two reflections in parallel or intersecting lines
- Determine whether a regular polygon tessellates using interior angle measures
- Classify tessellations as regular, semiregular, uniform, or not uniform
- Identify lines of symmetry and rotational symmetry in figures
- Find the order and magnitude of rotational symmetry

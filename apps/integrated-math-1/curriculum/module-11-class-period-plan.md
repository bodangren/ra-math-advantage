## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 11 (Angles and Geometric Figures) is broad, covering angle relationships, 2D figures, transformations in the plane, 3D solids, 2D representations of 3D figures, and precision/accuracy.
- Lessons 11-1 through 11-4 focus on angle relationships and rigid motions; Lessons 11-5 through 11-8 shift to measurement, solids, representations, and precision.

## Why This Split Works

- `11-1` and `11-2` (angle relationships) form a tight algebraic geometry unit ‚Äî angle bisector, linear pairs, vertical angles, complementary/supplementary.
- `11-3` (2D figures) is a bridge lesson ‚Äî perimeter/area from coordinate grids and models, uses Distance Formula implicitly.
- `11-4` (transformations) is computationally engaging with clear coordinate rules ‚Äî two days allows exploration of all three rigid motions.
- `11-5` and `11-6` (3D solids and their 2D representations) are conceptually related ‚Äî building from/to orthographic views and nets.
- `11-7` and `11-8` (precision and significant figures) are complementary lessons on measurement quality.
- The non-instruction days provide spaced practice for the diverse skills in this module.

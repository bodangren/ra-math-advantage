# Module 9: Statistics

## Overview
This module develops statistical reasoning from descriptive statistics to data interpretation and representation. Students calculate and compare measures of center and spread, construct and analyze data displays, evaluate sampling methods and survey designs, and organize categorical data using two-way tables. The module emphasizes understanding what statistics reveal and how data collection choices can affect conclusions.

## Skills Developed
- Calculate mean, median, mode, and percentile rank
- Construct and interpret dot plots, bar graphs, and histograms
- Distinguish between discrete and continuous data
- Identify sample bias and biased survey questions
- Calculate range, interquartile range, and standard deviation
- Construct and interpret box plots and five-number summaries
- Analyze the effect of outliers on measures of center and spread
- Analyze how adding or multiplying by a constant affects statistics
- Construct and interpret two-way frequency tables, relative frequency tables, and conditional relative frequency tables
- Determine associations between categorical variables

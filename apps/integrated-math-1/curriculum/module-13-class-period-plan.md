## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Most instruction days use **2-3 worked examples**.
- Module 13 (Transformations and Symmetry) is the shortest IM1 module with 6 lessons, covering reflections, translations, rotations, compositions, tessellations, and symmetry.
- Lessons 13-1 through 13-4 focus on single and composite rigid transformations; Lessons 13-5 and 13-6 apply transformations to tessellations and symmetry.

## Why This Split Works

- `13-1` (reflections) splits naturally between graphing images and finding coordinates of reflected points.
- `13-2` (translations) covers both identification of translations and applying translation vectors.
- `13-3` (rotations) requires the translate-rotate-translate-back method for arbitrary centers ‚Äî needs two days.
- `13-4` (compositions) introduces glide reflections and the key insight that two reflections compose to a translation or rotation.
- `13-5` (tessellations) connects transformations to coverage patterns ‚Äî lighter lesson but conceptually rich.
- `13-6` (symmetry) is the natural endpoint ‚Äî applying rigid motions to understand symmetry types.
- The non-instruction days support the visual and constructive nature of transformation learning.

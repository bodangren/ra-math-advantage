# Module 10: Tools of Geometry

## Overview
This module establishes the foundational language and tools of geometry, from undefined terms to precise measurements. Students explore the axiomatic structure of geometry, learn to name and characterize points, lines, and planes, apply segment relationships including the Segment Addition Postulate, and use the Distance and Midpoint Formulas to find lengths and coordinates. The module bridges geometric reasoning with algebraic computation on the coordinate plane.

## Skills Developed
- Understand axiomatic systems and distinguish axioms from conclusions
- Differentiate between synthetic and analytic geometry
- Identify and name points, lines, planes, collinear points, and coplanar points
- Apply the Segment Addition Postulate and solve algebraic equations involving segment lengths
- Find distances on a number line and in the coordinate plane using the Distance Formula
- Determine whether segments are congruent by comparing lengths
- Partition segments using fractional distances and ratios
- Apply the Midpoint Formula and find missing endpoints
- Solve real-world problems involving distances, locations, and proportional divisions

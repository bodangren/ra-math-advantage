# Module 9 Class-Period Plan

This document is the audited class-period breakdown for Module 9.

It should be treated as the canonical planning source for Module 9 class periods unless a later audit supersedes it.

It follows the planning model in `curriculum/course-spec.md`:

- the **class period** is the atomic planning unit
- `instruction` days must map to `conductor/course-objectives.md`
- `instruction` days must name the specific worked examples they use
- `mastery`, `jigsaw`, `review`, and `test` days are marked primarily by day type

## Notes

- This is a **planning model**, not a literal calendar.
- Module 9 has 14 course objectives and 16 instruction days, so two objective clusters intentionally span more than one class period.
- The extra instructional space is used for:
  - a second day on graphing and analyzing sine/cosine functions
  - a second day on inverse trigonometric functions and angle-measure interpretation
- This module is content-heavy and example-dense, so several lessons are split into three distinct instructional beats.
- This plan has been checked against the current repaired lesson source files and worked-example numbering.

## Why This Split Works

- `9-1` divides naturally into angle-position work and degree/radian/arc-length work.
- `9-2` needs three days because acute-angle trig, general-angle trig, and reference-angle reasoning are distinct instructional beats.
- `9-4` deserves three days because graph analysis, graph construction, and real-world sinusoidal modeling are materially different tasks.
- `9-5` and `9-6` each divide cleanly into two objective-based days.
- `9-7` benefits from two inverse-trig days so symbolic evaluation and applied angle-measure solving are not compressed into one period.

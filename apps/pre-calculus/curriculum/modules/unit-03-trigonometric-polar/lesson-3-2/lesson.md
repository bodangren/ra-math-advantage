# Lesson 3-2 — Sine, Cosine, and Tangent

Unit: 3
Topic: 3.2
AP Exam: assessed-on-ap-exam

## Explore

### Section: text

A point [P] lies on a circle of radius 5 centered at the origin. The ray from the origin through [P] makes an angle [\theta] with the positive x-axis. Suppose [P] has coordinates [(3, 4)].

Can you express the vertical position of [P] as a ratio involving the radius? What about the horizontal position? These ratios turn out to define three fundamental functions that connect angles to coordinates.

## Vocabulary

### Section: text

- **Standard position**: An angle is in standard position when its vertex is at the origin and one ray lies along the positive x-axis.
- **Terminal ray**: The second ray of the angle — the one that rotates from the positive x-axis.
- **Radian measure**: The ratio of the arc length to the radius. On the unit circle, the radian measure equals the arc length.
- **Unit circle**: A circle centered at the origin with radius 1.
- **sin [\theta]**: The ratio of the vertical displacement to the radius — [\sin \theta = y/r].
- **cos [\theta]**: The ratio of the horizontal displacement to the radius — [\cos \theta = x/r].
- **tan [\theta]**: The slope of the terminal ray — [\tan \theta = y/x] or [\sin \theta / \cos \theta].

## Learn

### Section: text

An angle in **standard position** has its vertex at the origin with one ray on the positive x-axis. The **terminal ray** is the rotated second ray. For any point [(x, y)] on the terminal ray at distance [r] from the origin:

\[\sin \theta = \frac{y}{r}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{x}{r}, \quad \tan \theta = \frac{y}{x}\]

Positive angles are measured counterclockwise; negative angles are measured clockwise.

**Radian measure** is defined as arc length divided by radius. A full rotation is [2\pi] radians (360°), a half rotation is [\pi], and a quarter rotation is [\pi/2]. On the **unit circle** where [r = 1], the definitions simplify:

\[\sin \theta = y, \quad \cos \theta = x, \quad \tan \theta = \frac{y}{x}\]

The tangent is undefined when [x = 0] (the terminal ray is vertical), which occurs at [\theta = \pi/2, 3\pi/2, \ldots].

## Worked Example

### Section: text

**Example 1:** Point [P] is on a circle of radius 5 in standard position at coordinates [(3, 4)]. Find [\sin \theta], [\cos \theta], and [\tan \theta].

**Solution:**
\[\sin \theta = \frac{y}{r} = \frac{4}{5}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{x}{r} = \frac{3}{5}, \quad \tan \theta = \frac{y}{x} = \frac{4}{3}\]

**Example 2:** Point [P(x, y)] is on a circle. [Q], [R], and [S] are reflections of [P] across the y-axis, the origin, and the x-axis respectively. Find their coordinates.

**Solution:**
- [Q = (-x, y)] (reflect across y-axis)
- [R = (-x, -y)] (reflect across origin)
- [S = (x, -y)] (reflect across x-axis)

**Example 3:** The terminal ray of angle [\theta] lies on the line [y = -3x] in quadrant II. Find [\sin \theta], [\cos \theta], and [\tan \theta].

**Solution:** In quadrant II, [x < 0] and [y > 0]. Choose [P = (-1, 3)]. Then [r = \sqrt{(-1)^2 + 3^2} = \sqrt{10}].
\[\sin \theta = \frac{3}{\sqrt{10}}, \quad \cos \theta = \frac{-1}{\sqrt{10}}, \quad \tan \theta = \frac{3}{-1} = -3\]

**Example 4:** Convert to radians: (a) [1/6] of a full rotation counterclockwise, (b) two full rotations clockwise.

**Solution:**
- (a) [\frac{1}{6} \times 2\pi = \frac{\pi}{3}]
- (b) [-2 \times 2\pi = -4\pi]

## Guided Practice

### Section: text

**Problem 1:** A circle has radius 7 and point [P(-5, y)] in quadrant II lies on it. Find [y], then compute [\sin \theta] and [\tan \theta].

**Hint:** Use [x^2 + y^2 = r^2] to find [y]. Remember [y > 0] in quadrant II.

**Problem 2:** Sketch the angle [\frac{3\pi}{4}] in standard position. In which quadrant does the terminal ray land?

**Hint:** [\pi/2 < 3\pi/4 < \pi], so the angle is in quadrant II.

**Problem 3:** Point [P] is on the unit circle at coordinates [(\frac{1}{2}, \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2})]. Find [\sin \theta], [\cos \theta], and [\tan \theta].

**Hint:** On the unit circle, [\sin \theta = y] and [\cos \theta = x].

## Reflection

### Section: text

**Exit Ticket:** Point [P] is on the terminal ray of angle [\theta] with coordinates [(-5, 12)]. Find [\sin \theta], [\cos \theta], and [\tan \theta].

**Lesson Summary:** The trigonometric functions sine, cosine, and tangent connect angles to coordinates via [\sin \theta = y/r], [\cos \theta = x/r], and [\tan \theta = y/x]. On the unit circle, these simplify to [\sin \theta = y] and [\cos \theta = x]. Radian measure expresses angles as arc-length-to-radius ratios, and the reflection pattern [(x, y) \to (-x, y) \to (-x, -y) \to (x, -y)] determines sign rules across quadrants.

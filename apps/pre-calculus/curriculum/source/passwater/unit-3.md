## Topic 3.1: Periodic Phenomena

### Notes Content (3 pages + 1 FRQ task model page)

**Vocabulary:** A **periodic** relationship occurs when output values demonstrate a repeating pattern over successive equal-length intervals. The **period** is the length of x-values for one complete cycle.

**Key equation:** If period of h is 5, then h(x) = h(x − 5) for all x.

**FRQ 3 Task Model introduced on notes page 5–6.** The exact problem:
- Context: Large clock mounted to vertical wall. 8-inch-long minute hand. Center is 120 inches above floor. At t = 0, minute hand pointed directly up. Clock runs twice as fast — next time at 12 is t = 30 minutes.
- Part (A): Determine possible coordinates (t, h(t)) for five labeled points F, G, J, K, P on the sinusoidal graph.
- Part (B)(i): Multiple choice — on interval (t₁, t₂), which is true about h?
- Part (B)(ii): Describe how the rate of change of h is changing over (t₁, t₂).

### Worked Examples

- **Example 1:** Function f periodic with period 4. Draw two additional periods.
- **Example 2:** Graph of periodic function g. Find the least possible period.
- **Example 3:** Using g from Example 2, find g(14), g(72), g(−17).
- **Example 4:** h periodic with period 5. Table: x = 1, 3, 4, 7; h(x) = −2, 0, 3, 2. Find h(−2), h(6), h(h(9)), h(5k − 3).
- **Example 5:** Periodic f with domain all reals. Find all inputs yielding output of 1.

### Worksheet Problems

**Worksheet A (4 pages):** Period identification from graphs, evaluating periodic functions at specific inputs (including compositions like f(f(10))), general form evaluations (e.g., f(7k + 4)).
- Problem 9: Table: x = −3, 0, 1, 10; f(x) = 4, 2, −1, 7. Period = 7. Find f(8), f(−6), f(f(10)), f(7k + 4).
- Problem 10: Table: x = −6, 0, 3, 7; g(x) = 1, 4, −2, 5. g(x + 10) = g(x). Find g(27), g(−7), g(g(10)), g(10k − 3).
- Problem 11 (FRQ 3 Model): Toy car on circular track. Center 20 inches from wall. At t = 0, distance is 0 inches. Lap every 8 seconds.

**Worksheets B–G: All FRQ 3 Model problems with physical contexts:**
- **B:** Steve on seesaw. 24 inches at t = 0. Highest: 60 inches. Cycle every 3 seconds.
- **C:** Prize wheel. Center 30 inches from point A. 4 rotations per second. At t = 0, point A at lowest.
- **D:** Boat motor blades. 50 rotations/second. Point B on tip, 8 inches from center. Center 18 inches below water line.
- **D2:** Boat motor. 60 rotations/second. B is 6 inches from center. Center 20 inches below water line.
- **D3:** Boat motor. 80 rotations/second. B is 9 inches from center. Center 22 inches below water line.
- **E:** Merry-go-round. Counterclockwise, one rotation every 3 seconds. B on edge, 4 feet from center. Center 10 feet from fence.
- **F:** Ferris Wheel. Clockwise, one rotation every 30 seconds. B is 40 feet from center. Center 50 feet above ground.
- **G:** "Box O' Fun" toy. Table 36 inches above ground. Crank center 9 inches above table. At t = 0, highest point is 50 inches from ground. One rotation every 4 seconds.

Each FRQ worksheet follows the same structure: Part (A) determine coordinates of 5 labeled points; Part (B)(i) multiple choice on behavior; Part (B)(ii) describe rate of change.

## Topic 3.2: Sine, Cosine, and Tangent

### Notes Content (Parts I–III, ~10 pages)

**Part II:** Evaluating at standard angles (multiples of π/6, π/4, π/3, π/2). Reference angles and quadrant signs.

**Part III:** Tangent = sin/cos. Tangent values at standard angles.

## Topics 3.4–3.5: Sine/Cosine Graphs and Sinusoidal Functions

### Notes Content (~6 pages)

- Graph y = sin(x) and y = cos(x): amplitude = 1, period = 2π, midline y = 0
- General sinusoidal: y = A sin(B(x − C)) + D or y = A cos(B(x − C)) + D
- Amplitude = |A|, period = 2π/|B|, phase shift = C, vertical shift = D

### Worksheets A–B

- Graph labeling, equation writing from parameters, equation writing from graphs.

## Topic 3.8: The Tangent Function

### Notes Content (~8 pages)

- tan(x) = sin(x)/cos(x)
- Period = π, vertical asymptotes at x = π/2 + nπ
- Odd function: tan(−x) = −tan(x)
- No amplitude (unbounded)

### Worksheets A–B

- Graphing tangent, evaluating, identifying asymptotes.

## Topic 3.9: Inverse Trigonometric Functions

### Notes Content (~2 pages)

- arccos: [−1, 1] → [0, π]
- arctan: all reals → (−π/2, π/2)
- Composition: sin(arccos(3/5)) using reference triangle

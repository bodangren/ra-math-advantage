## CED Learning Objectives

- LO 3.3.A: Evaluate sine and cosine at standard angles on the unit circle. Use symmetry and geometric reasoning to determine exact values.

## CED Essential Knowledge

- EK 3.3.A.1: The coordinates of point P on a circle of radius r are (r cos θ, r sin θ). On the unit circle, cos θ and sin θ are the x- and y-coordinates.
- EK 3.3.A.2: Special angles (π/6, π/4, π/3 and their reflections) have exact sin and cos values derivable from equilateral and isosceles right triangles inscribed in the unit circle.
- EK 3.3.A.3: The Pythagorean theorem underlies the unit circle — sin²θ + cos²θ = 1 for all θ — and is used to find coordinates when one is known.

## Passwater Scaffolding Notes

Passwater connects Topic 3.3 to Topic 3.2 by rearranging: from cos θ = x/r and sin θ = y/r, multiplying gives x = r cos θ and y = r sin θ, so "the coordinates of point P are (r cos θ, r sin θ)." This reframing is critical — students move from computing ratios to using trig as coordinate generators.

The unit circle construction is done geometrically: an equilateral triangle inscribed in the unit circle gives angle π/6 with y-coordinate 1/2. The Pythagorean theorem gives x = √3/2, so cos π/6 = √3/2 and sin π/6 = 1/2. For π/4, an isosceles right triangle gives x = y, and 2x² = 1 yields x = √2/2. For π/3, coordinates are (1/2, √3/2).

Passwater's "Two Important Notes": (1) "Notice, we found two values of x when solving with the Pythagorean theorem. This is because there is another point in quadrant II with the y-coordinate of 1/2." — This connects to the 5π/6 angle. (2) "The Pythagorean theorem plays an important and interesting role with trigonometric functions." — Foreshadows the Pythagorean identity in 3.12.

The critical memorization statement: "The unit circle above displays the coordinates of the points in quadrant I. These are the points that must be memorized so that we can use them for the points in the remaining quadrants."

Scaffolding moves: coordinates on arbitrary circles → equilateral/isosceles triangle construction → unit circle quadrant I values → reflections to all quadrants → exact evaluation.

## Guided Practice

**Example 1:** Circle radius 4 with equilateral triangle PQO. Which gives coordinates of Q? Options: (4 cos π/3, 4 sin π/3), (4 cos 5π/6, 4 sin 5π/6), (4 cos 2π/3, 4 sin 2π/3), (4 cos 2π/3, −4 sin 2π/3). Answer uses the fact that the angle at Q is 2π/3.

**Example 2:** Circle radius r, line y = −x, P is (6√2, −6√2). Find r. Since y = −x, tan θ = −1. r² = (6√2)² + (−6√2)² = 72 + 72 = 144, so r = 12.

**Example 3:** Unit circle with points S, P, Q, R. Find: a) cos 0 = 1, b) sin 0 = 0, c) cos π/2 = 0, d) sin π/2 = 1, e) cos π = −1, f) sin π = 0, g) cos 3π/2 = 0, h) sin 3π/2 = −1.

**Example 4:** Unit circle with equilateral triangles. Given P at π/6, find coordinates and angle measures for Q, R, S using symmetry.

**Example 7:** Find exact values: a) sin(2π/3) = √3/2, b) cos(5π/4) = −√2/2, c) cos(11π/6) = √3/2, d) sin(7π/4) = −√2/2, e) cos(4π/3) = −1/2, f) sin(7π/6) = −1/2, g) cos π = −1, h) sin(3π/2) = −1.

## Independent Practice Description

Worksheet A: 24 problems. Problems 1–8: MC — coordinates on circles of radius 3, 9, 5, 15 with equilateral/isosceles triangles. Problems 9–12: Find exact values: sin(5π/6), cos(π/4), sin(π/3), cos(5π/6). Problems 13–24: Find exact values of various sin and cos expressions.

Worksheet B: 20 problems — "Find the following values without a calculator." Examples: sin(5π/3) = −√3/2, cos(3π/4) = −√2/2, sin(5π/6) = 1/2, cos(π/6) = √3/2, tan(11π/6) = −√3/3, cos(2π/3) = −1/2.

Worksheet C: 24 problems — same format with sin(5π/6), cos(π/4), sin(5π/4) = −√2/2, cos(11π/6) = √3/2, etc.

## Additional Notes

The memorization requirement for quadrant I unit circle values — (√3/2, 1/2) at π/6, (√2/2, √2/2) at π/4, (1/2, √3/2) at π/3 — is foundational for every subsequent topic in Unit 3. All exact value evaluation, equation solving, and identity work depends on fluency with these values.

## CED Learning Objectives

- LO 1.4.A: Identify and classify extrema and points of inflection of a polynomial function from its graph.

## CED Essential Knowledge

- EK 1.4.A.1: A polynomial function is any function representation equivalent to the form p(x) = aₙxⁿ + aₙ₋₁xⁿ⁻¹ + ⋯ + a₁x + a₀, where n is a positive integer, each aᵢ is a real number, and aₙ is nonzero.
- EK 1.4.A.2: A relative minimum or relative maximum occurs where a polynomial switches between decreasing and increasing. The absolute maximum is the greatest of all local maxima; the absolute minimum is the least of all local minima.
- EK 1.4.A.3: A point of inflection occurs where a function changes concavity. At a point of inflection, the rate of change switches between increasing and decreasing.

## Passwater Scaffolding Notes

- Topic introduction: Passwater begins with leading coefficient and degree identification from factored and expanded forms. Fill-in-the-blank: "The extrema of a graph are the minimums and maximums of a function. There are two types of extrema: relative (local) and absolute (global)."
- Key vocabulary (fill-in-the-blank): Leading term, degree, leading coefficient, relative minimum, relative maximum, absolute minimum, absolute maximum, point of inflection.
- Polynomial definition (fill-in-the-blank): "A polynomial function is any function representation equivalent to the analytical form p(x) = aₙxⁿ + aₙ₋₁xⁿ⁻¹ + ⋯ + a₂x² + a₁x + a₀, where n is a positive integer, aᵢ is a real number for each i from 1 to n, and aₙ is nonzero."
- Passwater's fun facts (fill-in-the-blank): "Between 2 real zeros of a polynomial, there must be at least one relative minimum or relative maximum." "Polynomials of odd degree must have either a relative minimum or a relative maximum."
- Point of inflection definition: "A point of inflection occurs when a function changes from concave up to concave down or from concave down to concave up. At a point of inflection, the rate of change of a function changes from increasing to decreasing or from decreasing to increasing."
- Scaffolding: Students classify extrema from graphs before working with equations. Passwater uses three graph-based examples (Example 2) where students label relative min, relative max, absolute min, absolute max.

## Guided Practice

- Example 1: Find the leading coefficient and degree:
  - a) f(x) = 3x⁴ + 2x − 7 → LC: 3, Degree: 4
  - b) y = ½x − 7x³ + 11 → LC: −7, Degree: 3
  - c) g(x) = 4 → LC: 4, Degree: 0
- Example 2: Three polynomial graphs — for each, identify relative minimum at x = __, relative maximum at x = __, absolute minimum = __ at x = __, absolute maximum = __ at x = __.
- Example 3: Graph of g shown. Find x-values where g has a point of inflection. For interval (3, 4): "Is the rate of change increasing or decreasing? Explain using features of g(x)."
- Example 4: C(t) = −1.37t⁵ + 4.218t⁴ − 0.357t² + 3 for 0 ≤ t ≤ 3. "At what time t does the number of cars change from increasing to decreasing?" — students use the graphing calculator to find the local maximum.

## Independent Practice Description

- AP-style tasks: Students identify extrema from graphs, determine if a polynomial must have a global min or max based on degree, and sketch polynomials with specified properties.
- Worksheet A Problem 15: f(x) = x⁴ − 3x⁵ + x + 6. "Determine if this polynomial must have a global minimum, global maximum, or neither. Explain reasoning." (Leading term −3x⁵: odd degree, negative LC → rises left, falls right → has a global max but no global min.)
- Worksheet A Problems 20–23: Sketch polynomials with specified properties — e.g., "f(x) has two points of inflection, one absolute maximum, and no absolute minimum."
- Worksheet A Problems 24–28: Given h(x) on [−1, 7], find open intervals where ROC is positive and decreasing, negative and decreasing, etc.
- Emphasis: Connect graph behavior to rate of change language. "ROC is positive and increasing" means the graph is going up and getting steeper.

## FRQ Expectations

- FRQ 1 (Function Concepts): Students may be asked to identify extrema from a graph, describe intervals of increase/decrease, and identify points of inflection. Subskill: classify extrema type.
- FRQ 3 (Function Concepts from a Graph): Passwater's format with five labeled points — students describe function behavior and rate of change on intervals.
- Common assessment item: "On which interval is f increasing and the graph of f concave down?" requires combining increase/decrease with concavity.
- Key AP note: On the AP Exam, FRQ 3 will appear with a graph and labeled points — students must describe function behavior and rate of change on specific intervals.
- AP mathematical practices targeted:
  - 1.B: Identify information from multiple representations (graph to verbal).
  - 2.A: Identify information from mathematical representations.
  - 3.B: Justify conclusions with mathematical reasoning (classify extrema, explain inflection).

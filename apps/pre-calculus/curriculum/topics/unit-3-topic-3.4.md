## CED Learning Objectives

- LO 3.4.A: Identify and describe the key features of the graphs of sine and cosine functions, including period, frequency, amplitude, and midline.

## CED Essential Knowledge

- EK 3.4.A.1: The graph of f(θ) = sin θ has midline y = 0, amplitude 1, period 2π, and frequency 1/(2π). It oscillates between concave down and concave up.
- EK 3.4.A.2: The graph of g(θ) = cos θ has the same properties: midline y = 0, amplitude 1, period 2π, frequency 1/(2π).
- EK 3.4.A.3: The midline is halfway between the maximum and minimum values. The amplitude is the distance from the midline to the maximum or minimum. The frequency is the reciprocal of the period.

## Passwater Scaffolding Notes

Passwater introduces graph properties as fill-in-the-blank: "The midline is halfway between the maximum and minimum values." → y = 0. "The amplitude is the distance from the midline to the maximum (or minimum)." → a = 1. "The frequency is the reciprocal of the period." → 1/(2π). A key behavioral note: "The graph of f(θ) = sin θ oscillates between concave down and concave up."

The connection between sin and cos is made: "The sine and cosine functions are both sinusoidal functions because g(θ) = cos θ = sin(θ + π/2)." This frames Topic 3.5 (sinusoidal functions) as a generalization.

Scaffolding moves from reading period/frequency/amplitude/midline off a graph (Examples 1–2) to determining properties from max/min point coordinates (Example 3: h(θ) has max at (π, 8), min at (3π, −2)), to the FRQ 3 Revisited with the clock context from 3.1 now adding period/frequency/amplitude/midline and interval behavior analysis.

Topics 3.4 and 3.5 are typically taught together (Passwater combines them with a single set of notes and worksheets). The pacing treats reading graph properties (3.4) and identifying sinusoidal functions (3.5) as one instructional block.

## Guided Practice

**Example 1:** Graph of f(θ) shown. "Find the period, frequency, amplitude, and midline." Students read max/min values and horizontal distance from the graph.

**Example 3:** "The sinusoidal function h(θ) has a maximum at the point (π, 8). The first minimum after this maximum occurs at the point (3π, −2). Find period, frequency, amplitude, and midline."
- Midline = (8 + (−2))/2 = 3
- Amplitude = (8 − (−2))/2 = 5
- Period = 2(3π − π) = 4π
- Frequency = 1/(4π)

**Example 4 — FRQ 3 Revisited:** Same clock from 3.1 (8-inch hand, 120 inches above floor, period 30 min). Part (A): determine coordinates for F, G, J, K, P. Part (B): find period, frequency, amplitude, midline. Part (C): find two intervals where h is both decreasing and concave up.

## Additional Notes

Passwater pairs Topics 3.4 and 3.5 as a single instructional block with combined worksheets. The quiz after these topics tests both graph property identification and sinusoidal function recognition. The interval behavior vocabulary — "increasing at an increasing rate," "decreasing at a decreasing rate," "concave up," "concave down" — is essential for FRQ 3 Part (B)(ii) responses where students must describe how the rate of change is changing.

The concavity analysis is new for many students: a sinusoidal graph is concave down above the midline and concave up below it. This means on any interval where the function is above the midline and decreasing, it is both decreasing and concave down — a combination that appears frequently in FRQ 3.

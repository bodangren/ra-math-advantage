## Lesson Progression Assessment

**Result: LOGICAL AND WELL-STRUCTURED**

The module follows a coherent instructional arc through statistics and data analysis:

1. **9-1 Measures of Center** — Introduces mean, median, mode, and percentile rank as ways to describe the "typical" value.
2. **9-2 Representing Data** — Teaches students to construct and choose among dot plots, bar graphs, and histograms based on data type.
3. **9-3 Using Data** — Shifts focus to data collection quality: sampling bias, survey question bias, and misleading graphs.
4. **9-4 Measures of Spread** — Adds range, five-number summary, box plots, IQR, and standard deviation to describe variability.
5. **9-5 Distributions of Data** — Combines visual displays with statistics to analyze shape (symmetric vs. skewed) and outlier effects.
6. **9-6 Comparing Sets of Data** — Applies transformations and comparative statistics to analyze and contrast two data sets.
7. **9-7 Summarizing Categorical Data** — Introduces two-way tables, joint/marginal/conditional relative frequencies, and association.

The flow moves smoothly from **describing center → displaying data → evaluating data quality → measuring spread → analyzing shape → comparing data sets → categorical summarization**.
